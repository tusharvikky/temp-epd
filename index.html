Plating

Electronic Product Design

Plating

The common name for surface-covering techniques in which a metal is deposited onto a conducting surface.

Electro'plating'

The application of a metal coating to a metallic -or- other conducting surface by an electrochemical process.

Why? Electroplate?

  • Alter their appearance
  • Provide a protective coating
  • Give special properties

Electroplating categories

  1. Sacrificial coating: For protection, denotes " sacrifice" themselves in the act of protecting.
  2. Decorative: adding attractive appearance.
  3. Engineering coating: a.k.a " functional" coating, for solderability, wear resistance, conductivity, etc.
  4. Minor metals : small metals easily plated but have limited application.
  5. ' Unusual' metals : rarely electroplated, require special condn like non-aqueous solutions.

Types

Racking
Barrel
Brush

Racking -or- wiring

  • individual parts are grouped with electrical continuity.
  • improves productivity, which in-turn reduces pricing.

However:

  • Requires each part be in physical, electrical contacy w/ power source.

Barrel plating

  • Provided with gold, silver, nickel, tin & copper.
  • It does not demand very-high volume and more productive than rack plating.

However:

  • It tumbles the parts, and can easily damage the part shape.

Brush plating

  • a.k.a selective plating, of concentrated plating on selected areas w/o immersion tanks.
  • Electrical contact is made by brushing.

Copper plating

  1. PCB is clamped in racks.  Immersed in a series of chemical baths that cleans the circuitry.
  2. Then immersed in copper plating solution. 
  3. Copper is plated of specified thickness on surface and holes.
  4. Process can be automated which moves the racked planels through series of baths.
  5. Further electroplated with tin/lead ( sholder)

Electroless plating

  1. Electroless Nickel plating, reduction of Nickel ion in aqueous solution and deposition of nickel metal w/o use of electrical energy.
  2. Driving force for reduction is supplied by a chemical reducing agent in solution.
  3. Hence, it offers uniform plating all over the part's shape.

However,

  • Less lifespan of chemicals and is a costly waste treatment process.

Gold plating

  1. Provide corrosive-resistant layer, typically is electrical connectors and printed circuit board.
  2. Nickel and gold layer are deposited by electroplating where Nickel provides mechanical backing for gold layer.

However,

  • At high freq, skin effect causes higher losses of nickel.
  • And nickel layer length can be shortened by factor of three in 1 GHz band.
